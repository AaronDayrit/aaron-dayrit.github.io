WEB SECURITY:

TECH COMMUNICATIONS

OCTOBER 4, 2021

Web communications can often be unsafe. Your information is the prime target of most cybercriminals, and it's important to be secure. To be secure is to be protected from anything that can cause harm to you and your data; this could be a vulnerability or an attack.

Communication Encryption

Encryption is the primary way of keeping information inaccessible to unwanted criminals. Encryption converts plaintext into an unreadable format(cyphertext). Because of encryption, sensitive information communicated by users and servers have an extra layer of protection against criminals. Even when accessed, the data will stay encrypted or unreadable.

SSL

SSL (Secure Socket Layer) is used to establish secure communications between networked computers. It's a protocol that creates an encrypted link between the networked devices to keep information protected. SSL uses explicit connections to encrypt data transferred; This is manually enabled by the via command.

TLS

TLS (Transport Layer Security) is the successor to SSL. TLS is the modern version of SSL used most recommended for transferring data. It uses implicit connections to automatically enable SSL encryption at all times after an established connection.

Common Webpage Security

There are more malicious websites than there are legitimate. Recent years have proven that malicious websites have been bridging the gap to look more believable. That's why it's important to distinguish the difference with various key points.

HTTP

HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) is a protocol used for transferring resources. Web communication was the purpose of HTTP between web browsers and servers. All data transmitted by HTTP is not encrypted. Because of this flaw, intercepting data was often easy and common. HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) works as an extension to HTTP. HTTPS encrypts information with TLS encryption. HTTPS both encrypts website requests and responses automatically using TLS encryption. HTTPS-enabled websites have lock icons on search bars of most web browsers. The website prefix will also be present at the beginning of the URL, whether it's HTTP or HTTPS.

Website Policies

Laws in various countries like Australia and Canada have been requiring websites to establish their policies. Website privacy policies are a specific section of a website that indicates what data the webpage collects from you. These policies show that a website is complying with the laws provided by countries. Around the world, countries' precautions to policies may vary with the information required, so it's still a good idea to keep an open mind.
