PUMPKIN CARVING:

THE PROCESS

OCTOBER 4, 2021

Carving pumpkins are fun and simple activity to get yourself in that Halloween spirit! In this manual, you will learn the anatomy of a pumpkin, and how to transform it into a Jack- o'- Lantern.

List of equipment
  • Towel
  • Large kitchen knife
  • Small knife
  • Large bowl
  • Newspaper
  • Dry erase marker/Pencil
  • Lighter
  • Candles

Anatomy

Outer-Layer

The outer layer includes the skin and cavity of the pumpkin. This is the hard layer/skin you see around the pumpkin. Using knives is the primary way to cut through this section.

Inner-Layer

The inner layer of a pumpkin is much softer than the outer layer. This layer contains the seeds and filling of a pumpkin. Spoons, remove this section from the pumpkin.

Before you design your pumpkin, you need a blank canvas. These are the steps.

  1. Start with handling the pumpkin on a solid surface to cut on, with some newspaper. Using a writing tool (Marker/pencil), mark a circle on the top of the pumpkin (around the stem).
  2. With the stem facing to the side and medium knife in hand, carefully cut downwards, and let the sharp knife do the work. The top will resemble a lid and your pumpkin like a pot.
  3. With the pumpkin, you can see an excess outer layer where you've just cut. Carefully remove it using your small knife by cutting a circle. This will reveal the seeds and inner layer of the pumpkin.
  4. With a spoon or your hand, you can remove all of the inner layers of the pumpkin and set it aside in the bowl to set aside. If you're planning on not using it, you can throw it away. Try to clean out most of the inside, having only the outer shell left.
  5. From here, you're left with a canvas to create whatever design you would like!

Now you have a completely blank canvas to create your masterpiece. Here are the steps to design.

  1. Set the lid of the pumpkin to the side (stem section). With a writing tool (marker or pencil), you can begin to draw a design. Keep in mind that more angular is much easier to cut compared to complex curves.
  2. Once a design is complete, you can begin to carve. Firmly hold the pumpkin with your off-hand. With a small knife, you can cut your design out. Use a rocking motion to slice through the hard skin. When space is limited, you can use an up-and-down motion to cut. Just keep in mind to keep your fingers out of the direction of the knife when cutting.
  3. Remove the excess pieces of pumpkin from the inside, outwards from the pumpkin.
  4. If there are still marker/pen marks present, you can easily wipe them off with a wet towel.

If you’ve followed the steps in this manual correctly, congrats! You’ve made your first Jack o’ Lantern. It wasn’t too hard, right?
